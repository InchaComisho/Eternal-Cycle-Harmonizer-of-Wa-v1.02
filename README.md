# Eternal Cycle — Harmonizer of Wa — v1.02  
## A Civilization-Tuning Action RPG About Preventing Planetary Collapse

A next-generation action RPG concept that combines:

- fast-paced 3-character combat  
- civilization-tuning simulation  
- planet-scale hex world systems  
- dynamic ecological collapse  
- time loops  
- forgive-or-execute decision structures  
- long-term sustainability mechanics  

Unlike traditional RPGs focused on “defeating evil,” Eternal Cycle asks a different question:

> **How can a civilization survive without collapsing?**

***

## 🌍 Core Concept

In Eternal Cycle, the player is not:

- a chosen hero  
- a conqueror  
- a ruler  
- a savior  

Instead, the player acts as:

> **A Harmonizer of Civilization**

A being who intervenes in collapsing regions, ecological disasters, ideological extremism, wars, pollution, and Void corruption in order to stabilize planetary circulation.

The goal is not domination.  
The true objective is:

> **Preventing the world from reaching irreversible civilizational collapse.**

***

## ⚖️ Core Philosophy

Most RPGs operate on a simple structure:

> Defeat evil → Save the world  

Eternal Cycle does not.

In this world, many bosses are not inherently evil.  
They are:

- the result of ecological collapse  
- resource wars  
- ideological extremism  
- pollution  
- survival pressure  
- circulation failure  
- civilizational imbalance  

In other words:

> **Bosses are manifestations of broken systems.**

The game is built around understanding:

- why civilizations collapse  
- how imbalance spreads  
- how systems decay  
- how harmony can be restored  

***

## 🌌 Execution and Forgiveness System

One of the core systems is the choice between:

### Execution

Immediate removal of threats.

**Advantages**

- short-term safety  
- valuable resource rewards  
- rapid stabilization  

**Disadvantages**

- root causes remain  
- hatred and division persist  
- future collapse risk increases  

### Forgiveness

An attempt to reintegrate enemies back into planetary circulation.

**Requires**

- investigation  
- environmental recovery  
- understanding regional history  
- dialogue and negotiation  
- stabilizing surrounding regions  
- dismantling harmful structures  

**Advantages**

- long-term stability  
- restored trade  
- allied civilizations  
- ecological recovery  
- Void suppression  

**Risks**

- betrayal  
- re-corruption  
- renewed collapse  

In short:

- **Execution** creates temporary order.  
- **Forgiveness** creates long-term circulation.  

However, forgiveness is not always the correct answer.  
The player must understand the actual causes behind each collapse.

***

## 🌍 Planetary Hex Map System

Eternal Cycle does not use a traditional seamless open world.  
Instead, the game uses:

> **A planetary-scale hex map system.**

This allows civilization, ecology, pollution, and circulation to evolve dynamically across the world.

***

## 🧭 Hex Map Scale

**Small Prototype**  
6 × 12 = 72 hexes  
Suitable for:

- tutorial worlds  
- islands  
- regional testing  

**Medium Scale**  
12 × 24 = 288 hexes  
Suitable for:

- continents  
- multiple nations  
- ocean systems  

Recommended for early playable versions.

**Standard Planet**  
18 × 36 = 648 hexes  
Suitable for:

- full planetary simulation  
- global trade systems  
- multi-civilization balance  

**Large Planet**  
24 × 48 = 1152 hexes  
Suitable for:

- advanced ecological simulation  
- large-scale dynamic civilizations  
- deep environmental systems

- greed

## ⚔️ Action RPG Combat System

### 3-Character Real-Time Combat

Core features:

- fast-paced combat  
- QTE chains  
- BREAK system  
- perfect dodge mechanics  
- combo systems  
- cooperative ultimates  
- hack-and-slash progression  

Unlike many modern action RPGs, weak enemies are designed to feel **satisfying** to defeat.  
Combat emphasizes flow and momentum rather than artificially inflated enemy durability.

***

## 🔥 Skill Direction System

Each class branches into four combat directions:

- **Single Target** – boss BREAK and burst damage  
- **Area** – crowd control and battlefield clearing  
- **Drain** – damage-to-healing circulation mechanics  
- **Reflection** – defense, barriers, and counter systems  

The same archetype changes depending on:

- race  
- ideology  
- civilization alignment  
- personality type  

***

## 🧙 Summoner System

Summoners use:

- spirits  
- floating weapons  
- automata  
- magical drones  
- ancient constructs  

However, **the stronger the summon, the shorter its duration.**  
This prevents summoners from becoming universally dominant.

**Summon Categories**

- **Single Target Summons** – short-duration burst damage  
- **Area Summons** – crowd clearing and suppression  
- **Drain Summons** – long-duration sustain support  
- **Reflection Summons** – barriers, counters, and defensive structures  

Attack summons disappear after:

- character switching  
- QTE activation  

Support summons remain longer but deal lower damage.

***

## 🌌 Boss Design Philosophy

Bosses are not isolated enemies.  
They are:

> **The central cores of collapsing civilizations.**

Boss fights include:

- the core entity  
- guards  
- environmental hazards  
- machines  
- corruption nodes  
- battlefield structures  

**Examples**

**Industrial Collapse Region**

- giant mining machine  
- defense drones  
- heat exhaust towers  
- pollution nodes  

**Deep Sea Collapse Region**

- mutated sea beast  
- parasites  
- corrupted ocean currents  
- small mutated marine life  

**Void Corruption Region**

- Void Core  
- Echo Soldiers  
- corrupted civilians  
- Void nodes  

Thus:

> **Enemy composition itself tells the story of civilization collapse.**  

Combat becomes a form of environmental storytelling.

***

## ⚫ Void and Eternal

### Void

Void is not merely “darkness.”  
It is:

> **A planetary circulation failure phenomenon, born from extreme imbalance.**

### Eternal

Eternal is not a standard final boss.  
It is:

> **The world’s forced correction mechanism.**

When civilizations fail completely, Eternal appears to reset the cycle.

Therefore, the true objective is not:

> “Defeating Eternal,”  

but:

> **Preventing Eternal from appearing at all.**

***

## ⏳ Time Loop System

When civilizations collapse:

- timelines reset  
- regions reorganize  
- civilizations change  
- causal structures shift  

However, **fragments remain.**

Examples:

- dreams  
- déjà vu  
- emotional resonance  
- ancient records  
- subconscious memories  

NPCs may vaguely remember previous cycles.  
Civilizations themselves carry echoes of past worlds.

***

## 🌍 True Ending

The true ending is achieved when:

> **The planet becomes self-sustaining without requiring Eternal’s intervention.**

This requires:

- stabilized civilizations  
- restored circulation  
- ecological balance  
- reduced Void influence  
- coexistence between races and ideologies  

At least one civilization from each race must evolve into:

> **A Pyramid Circulation Civilization**  

representing fully sustainable civilizational structures.

***

## 🌱 The Six Principles

The world is built around six foundational principles:

- **Natural Law** – the unchangeable rules of ecosystems, physics, and circulation  
- **Harmony** – the ability of different civilizations to coexist  
- **Circulation** – the continuous movement of energy, resources, and life  
- **Structure** – the frameworks preventing total collapse  
- **Order** – balance between freedom and stability  
- **Wa** – the integration and resonance of all principles into sustainable coexistence

## 🧬 Character Design Framework

Playable characters in Eternal Cycle are designed as a combination of:

> **Race × Ideology (Personality) × Job × Skill Direction × Rebuild**

### Race

Humans, Elves, Dwarves, Angels, Demons, and Beast Tribes each represent different civilizational philosophies, environmental adaptations, and historical trajectories.

### Ideology (Personality Alignment)

Each character’s worldview affects how they perceive civilization:

- **Conservative** – prefers stability, preservation, and low-risk gradual change  
- **Neutral** – observant, flexible, and relatively unaligned  
- **Reformist** – favors transformation, experimentation, and high-impact change  

This ideological axis affects:

- which quests resonate with them  
- how they respond to different civilizations  
- what kind of “balance” they naturally push the world toward  

### Job

Traditional combat roles adapted to a civilization-tuning world:

- frontliners  
- ranged attackers  
- summoners  
- supports  
- tanks  

Jobs define combat function, not power creep.

### Skill Direction

Each job branches into four **skill directions**:

- **Single Target** – focused BREAK and boss damage  
- **Area** – wave clearing and battlefield control  
- **Drain** – sustain, regeneration, and circulation support  
- **Reflection** – defense, counters, and barrier systems  

The same job can play very differently depending on its chosen direction.

### Rebuild

After progressing the story, players can **rebuild** existing characters:

- change their skill direction  
- adjust their role within the party  
- reinterpret the same character through a different build  

This allows players to explore:

- “What if this conservative knight became a reformist barrier support?”  
- “What if this neutral mage shifted into a high-risk, high-reward Drain build?”  

Eternal Cycle treats characters not as static units, but as **living extensions of civilizations and their choices.**

***

## 💠 Unlock & Monetization Philosophy

Eternal Cycle is designed around **fair progression** and **world-consistent monetization**.  
Monetization must not break the core fantasy of “observing and tuning civilization.”

### Playable Character Unlocks

- At the start, players have access to **six neutral-aligned characters.**  
  These represent observers who have not yet committed to any extreme ideology.

- As the world evolves and civilizations drift toward different balances,  
  **conservative-leaning and reformist-leaning characters** are gradually unlocked through progression and world states.

This means:

- the roster itself reflects **how the planet is changing**  
- different runs may unlock different characters first, depending on player choices

### Monetization Scope

Monetization, if implemented, is strictly limited to:

- **Skins** – visual variations, themed outfits, cosmetic armor  
- **Pets / visual companions** – purely aesthetic entities or minor out-of-combat flavor  
- **Time-savers** – mild quality-of-life boosts for repeat grinding (e.g., slightly improved drop rates, extra storage, cosmetic pass boosts)

Eternal Cycle **does not** sell:

- direct power upgrades  
- pay-to-win stat boosts  
- exclusive gameplay-critical characters or builds  

The core design rule:

> **Money may change how you look and how fast you repeat content,  
> but never whether your civilization survives.**

This keeps:

- the philosophical core intact  
- balance focused on choices and tuning, not spending  
- long-term sustainability aligned with the theme of the game itself  

Eternal Cycle is not:

- merely an action RPG  
- merely a civilization simulator  
- merely a fantasy game  

It is:

> **A civilization causality observation game.**

Players are not rulers.  
They are:

- observers  
- harmonizers  
- restorers of circulation  

The goal is not conquest.  
The goal is:

> **Building a civilization that no longer requires forced correction.**
